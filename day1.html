Emotional Meteorology Bureau Intro

Our mood changes every day, just like the weather. But unlike the weather, emotions are often difficult to describe, express, and understand by others. The Emotional Meteorological Bureau is a system that allows us to record the emotional state in "meteorological language". It is not a mood diary, but an experiment to translate feelings into visible structures. Here, feelings do not need to be explained or justified. Like the weather, they are naturally worth observing, recording and naming.

Emotional Attributes Forecast

1. Color Temperature

Intensity

Map daily emotional intensity (0–100) to the HSL color ring.
The stronger the emotion → the more saturated and bright the color.
Output: HSL chromatography map.

2. Weather Verbs

Emotion Texture

Avoid words like “happy” or “tired.”
Express emotions as meteorological verbs: drift, condense, fog, evaporate, brighten…
Output: Emotion Tag / Weather Verb.

3. Peak Emotion Time

Temporality

Record the day’s emotional highs and lows — like sunrise and sunset.
Output: Line chart.

4. Social Visibility

Visibility

Score 0–10: how visible you wish to be to others today.
Output: Radar chart.

5. Volatility Index

Variability

Measure emotional fluctuation through the day (max–min).
Output: Air pressure curve.

6. Precipitation Probability

Tears / Overflow / Release

Likelihood of emotional precipitation—how close emotions are to overflowing.
Output: Droplet density map.

7. Perceived Temperature

Warmth / Numbness / Chill

Represents the gap between internal emotion and external comfort.
Output: Thermal gradient.

8. Advisory

Caution / Alert / Acceptance

Reflect before emotional storms — slow down, breathe, and observe.
Output: Text alert.
